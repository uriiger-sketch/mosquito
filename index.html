🎯 How Detection Works ›

Each mosquito species beats its wings at a characteristic frequency. The app listens passively through your microphone, computes a real-time FFT spectrogram, and looks for narrow tonal peaks with the right harmonic structure.

SpeciesFreq (Hz)DiseaseRisk
Anopheles gambiae
~410MalariaCRITICAL
Aedes aegypti

Ae. albopictus
~668Dengue/Chik.HIGH
Culex quinquefasciatus
~453West NileMEDIUM

The classifier scores each species across four factors: SNR (35%), harmonic structure at 2f and 3f (30%), spectral tonality (20%), and peak sharpness (15%). A 5-frame temporal buffer (~200 ms) prevents false positives from brief tones.

🗺️ About the Map Data ›

Each circle on the map = one city-level vector presence record. Colour indicates species. Opacity reflects relative burden.

All data is from free, public, peer-reviewed sources. Radii are pre-validated to keep scatter on land (coastal cities ≤0.22°).

  • WHO World Malaria Report 2025 — 282M cases, 610K deaths in 2024. who.int
  • PAHO Dengue Bulletin 2024 — 14.4M Americas cases (record). Brazil 10M+. paho.org
  • WHO WER-10052 (2025) — Global dengue country breakdown.
  • CDC ArboNET 2024 — 1,466 US WNV cases. SD highest seroprevalence. cdc.gov
  • ECDC Mosquito Maps (May 2024) — EU Ae. albopictus distribution. ecdc.europa.eu
  • Kraemer et al. 2015 (eLife, DOI:10.7554/eLife.08347) — Global Aedes compendium.

Historical slider spans 1996 (Romania WNV) through 2024. All city centroids verified against OpenStreetMap.

🔒 Privacy & Federated Learning ›
  • No audio stored or transmitted. Microphone data is processed in real-time and immediately discarded.
  • No audio transmitted. Only gradient weight updates leave the device.
  • Differential Privacy: Gaussian mechanism with ε=1.0, δ=1e-5. Gradient L2 norm clipped to 1.0 before noise injection.
  • Anonymous device ID: Random 128-bit value, SHA-256 hashed before any upload. Never linked to identity.
  • FedAvg: Aggregates every 100 local steps, weighted by step count.
